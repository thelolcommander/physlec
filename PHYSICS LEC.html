01 · Moving inertia

Momentum

Momentum is a quantity that describes an object's resistance to stopping (a kind of"moving inertia").

It is represented by the symbol p (boldface) with a unit of kilogram-meter per second.

It is the product of an object's mass and velocity.

It is a vector quantity

02 · Moving force

Impulse

It is a vector quantity that describes the effect of a net force acting on an object (a kind of "moving force").

Impulse is represented by the symbol J (boldface) with a unit of Newton-second.

It is mathematically defined as the product of the average net force acting on an object and its duration.

J = F·Δt
Impulse40 N·s
Real life: same impulse, different sting

Catching a ball always absorbs the same impulse for a given speed the ball's momentum has to drop to zero either way. What changes is how long you take to do it, which changes the force your hand feels.

03 · The link

Impulse–Momentum Theorem

Momentum's unit (kg·m/s) and impulse's unit (N·s) are dimensionally identical — that's the tell that they're related. Starting from Newton's second law F = ma and substituting a = Δv/t, the impulse an object receives always equals its change in momentum.

J = mΔv = Δp

Try it: pick a force and duration, and a mass — watch the resulting velocity change come out consistent every time.
